# Project Title: Ashesi Life – Integrated Campus Life & Engagement Platform
Proposed Technology Stack: Flutter (for seamless performance on both iOS and Android
devices used by the Ashesi community).
Background Information on the Organization
Organization Name: Ashesi University
Purpose: Ashesi University is a leading private, non-profit university in Ghana, renowned for its
mission to educate ethical, entrepreneurial leaders in Africa. Ashesi's culture emphasizes a
vibrant campus life, student leadership, community engagement, and the holistic development of
its students beyond academics.
Principal Operations/Structure:
●
●
●
Core Operations: Delivering world-class education while fostering self-governance, and
a proactive community.
Academic Structure: Composed of majors in Business, Engineering, Law with Public
Policy, Computer Science, Management Information System, and Economics.
Campus Life Ecosystem: A dynamic environment supported by:
○
Student-Led Committees & Clubs: e.g., Student Council, Judicial Committee,
Sports Committee, Public Relations Committee and special interest clubs.
○
Administrative Departments: Student Life and Engagement, Logistics and
Facilities, and IT Services.
○
Student Services: Career Services, Counselling, Health Centre.
Target Market:
1. Primary: All Ashesi University students (undergraduate and graduate).
2. Secondary: Faculty, Staff, and Student Leaders (Committee/Club Executives).
3. Tertiary: Campus Administration and Department Heads.
Overview of App Operations, Functionalities, and Capabilities
The Ashesi Life app will be the official digital hub for campus engagement, communication, and
issue resolution. It aims to optimize information flow, empower student governance, and
simplify access to campus resources, directly supporting Ashesi's community-driven values.
Core Functional Requirements / Capabilities:
1. Unified Campus Event Hub & Discovery:
○
Centralized Calendar: A master calendar holding events from all recognized
clubs, committees, and official departments (Career talks, club meetings, guest
lectures, social events).
○
Personalized Feed & Alerts: Users can follow specific clubs/departments. Push
Notifications are sent for upcoming events from followed entities or for
high-priority announcements.
○
Event Details & RSVP: Detailed event pages with location, time, description,
and organizer info. Integrated RSVP system for planning.
2. Campus Issue Reporting & Resolution System:
●
Rich Report Submission:
○
Camera/Gallery Integration: Attach photos or videos as evidence.
○
Microphone: Optional voice note for additional description.
○
Transparent Tracking: Users can track the status of their reports
("Submitted", "Under Review", "Assigned to Facilities", "Resolved").
●
Categorized Reporting: Students can report issues like:
○
Facilities Maintenance: Broken furniture, electrical faults, plumbing
issues.
○
IT Problems: WiFi dead zones, lab computer issues.
○
Residence Life Concerns: Noise violations, common area issues.
○
Security & Safety: Malfunctioning lights, suspicious activity.
3. Committee & Club Management Portal:
●
Verified Organization Accounts: Official accounts for student council
committees and various departments of the school.
●
Communication Tools: Broadcast targeted push notifications to their followers.
●
Content Management:
○
Create and publish events directly to the main calendar.
○
Post announcements to their followers.
○
Upload and share resources (e.g., PDF guides, meeting minutes,
application forms).
4. Digital Campus Directory & Resource Access:
●
Searchable Directory: faculty, staff, and departments with contact details.
●
●
Quick Contact Integration: Phone Call and Email buttons for direct contact
from the directory listing.
Resource Repository: A categorized space where committees or departments can
make essential documents permanently available (e.g., Student Handbook, Club
Constitution templates).
5. Personal Student Dashboard:
●
Displays upcoming events from following organizations.
●
Shows the status of submitted issue reports.
Required Local Resources / Features:
●
●
●
●
●
●
Camera: For taking photos to attach to issue reports.
Push Notifications: For event reminders, announcement alerts, and updates on
reported issues.
Microphone (Optional): For adding voice notes to reports.
Phone Call and Email Integration: For seamless contact from the campus
directory.
Gallery Access: To upload existing photos for issue reports.
Background Task / Fetch: To periodically check for and deliver new
notifications even when the app is not active.
Non-Functional Requirements:
●
●
●
●
●
Usability & Inclusivity: The interface must be intuitive for all users, respecting
diverse levels of digital literacy. It must align with Ashesi's design standards and
be accessible.
Reliability: The issue reporting system must be robust, ensuring no reports are
lost. Notifications for urgent matters (security, critical announcements) must be
guaranteed.
Security & Privacy: Must use authenticated credentials for login. Sensitive user
data must be encrypted. Role-based access is crucial (e.g., only Facilities staff see
maintenance reports).
Performance: The app must feel fast and responsive, especially when loading the
event calendar and submitting reports.
Maintainability: The codebase should be well-structured for future Ashesi IT
teams or student developers to update and maintain.
